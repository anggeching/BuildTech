Teamwork in Technical Problem Solving

Every great innovation begins with a spark of an idea, and for our team of four, that spark ignited with the creation of a Shot Clock System. Our journey began with planning and designing, where we harnessed the power of Proteus to simulate our circuits. As the "brain" of the group, I took the lead in the circuit simulation, ensuring every connection and component was flawless.

Once the simulation proved successful, the real challenge began: the delegation of tasks for implementation. Unlike modern projects that rely heavily on programming, our work was entirely hands-on and manual, demanding both technical precision and strong teamwork. One group member mastered the art of etching, another ensured clean soldering, while I oversaw the intricate wiring. Together, we transformed a virtual design into a functional reality.

This project wasn’t just about building a timer—it was about building skills: from technical problem-solving to collaboration under pressure, we learned to trust each other’s strengths and turn challenges into success. With every soldered wire and etched circuit, we didn’t just create a working shot clock; we engineered a tangible milestone in our journey as aspiring engineers.
