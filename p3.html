Engineering Meets Creativity

Inspired by the Micromouse competition hosted by IEEE, our project involved designing and programming an Arduino Uno-based maze-solving robot under the guidance of Engr. Charles Juanillas. The challenge was to create a robot that could autonomously navigate a maze, detect obstacles, and find the optimal path to the exit. The project pushed us to blend both hardware and software in innovative ways, resulting in a hands-on experience with robotics, sensors, and control systems that would deepen our understanding of autonomous navigation.

This project began with the most fundamental stepâ€”the design of the robot's body. We envisioned a compact, lightweight structure that would allow the robot to swiftly move through the maze. The body needed to accommodate the necessary components, including motors for movement and sensors for detecting the maze walls. After careful planning and iterations, we finalized the design, ensuring stability and smooth navigation as key priorities.

Programming the robot was where the real fun happened. Using C++ on the Arduino IDE, we developed a series of algorithms that allowed the robot to detect obstacles and navigate the maze autonomously. This phase was a deep dive into Control Systems and Logic Implementation. We learned to master control structures such as loops, conditional statements, and functions in C++ to process sensor data and make real-time decisions. For instance, based on ultrasonic sensor readings, the robot could decide whether to turn, go forward, or stop, all while trying to find the most efficient route to the exit. The challenge of combining hardware and software in such a dynamic environment was both rewarding and educational, offering valuable insights into robotics and programming.
